The Airbnb Urban Stress Index (AUSI) is a composite indicator built from InsideAirbnb data. It measures the pressure short-term rentals put on housing, local wages and urban life, across Brussels, Budapest and Athens.

How it's built
4 weighted dimensions

AUSI combines Housing Pressure (35%), Platform Concentration (25%), Tourist Saturation (20%), and Price Exclusion (20%).

What it measures
Stress on cities

A high AUSI score means more housing removed from the long-term market, higher commercial host concentration, and nightly prices that exceed local daily wages.

How to read it
Relative, not absolute

0 = no detectable pressure. 100 = maximum pressure within this study. Brussels 18 Low. Budapest 63.8 High. Athens 79.5 Critical.
